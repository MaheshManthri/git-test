📌 Meaning of Profit Maximization

Profit Maximization is a fundamental business objective where a firm aims to earn the highest possible profit from its operations. It helps managers decide the most efficient level of production. Producing too little may waste opportunities, while producing too much may increase costs. Profit depends on the relationship between revenue and cost. Revenue comes from selling products, while cost arises from production. The goal is not maximum sales, but maximum profit. Profit maximization helps avoid unnecessary expenses. It supports long-term sustainability of the business. It guides pricing and output decisions. This concept is widely used in managerial economics and decision-making.

📐 Core Formulas Used

Total Revenue (TR)
TR = Price × Quantity

Total Cost (TC)
TC = Fixed Cost + Variable Cost

Profit (π)
π = Total Revenue − Total Cost

Profit Maximization Rule
Maximum profit occurs where profit (π) is highest.
