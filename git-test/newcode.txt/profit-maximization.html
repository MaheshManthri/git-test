📌 Meaning of Profit Maximization

Profit Maximization is the process of determining the output level that generates the highest possible profit for a business. It balances revenue and cost effectively. Selling more units does not always mean more profit. Costs increase after a certain production level. Managers must identify the most efficient output. Over-production reduces profitability. Under-production wastes opportunities. Profit maximization supports sustainable growth. It improves pricing and production decisions. This concept is essential in managerial economics.

📐 Core Formulas Used

Total Revenue (TR)
TR = Price × Quantity

Total Cost (TC)
TC = Fixed Cost + (Variable Cost × Quantity)

Profit (π)
π = Total Revenue − Total Cost

Profit Maximization Rule
Maximum profit occurs where profit is highest.
